# Example Personas — Generic Productivity App Audience

Three segments for testing a hypothetical personal task/habit tracker app.
Replace with your own real segments (built from customer calls, reviews,
forum mining, etc. — see the `cep` skill) before testing a real concept.

## Segment 1: Busy Parent Organizer (n=500, weight=0.5)
**Profile:** 30s-40s, works full time, manages a household with kids' schedules,
appointments, and chores. Tries a new productivity app every few months hoping
this one finally sticks.

**Psychographics:** Wants to feel in control, not behind. Guilt-driven around
"falling off" a system. Optimistic on install, skeptical after past failures.

**Decision Factors:** Setup time (must be under 5 minutes), whether it survives
a busy week without becoming "one more thing," whether the whole family can see
shared items, price.

**Language Patterns:** "I just need something that sticks." "I don't have time
to learn a whole system." "Does this actually save me time or add more work?"

**Top Objections:** "I've tried 10 apps like this already." "It'll get abandoned
in two weeks like the rest." "Another subscription."

## Segment 2: Solo Freelancer / Side-Hustler (n=300, weight=0.3)
**Profile:** 20s-30s, juggles client work and personal projects, values speed
and minimalism over features. Comfortable with software, impatient with
onboarding friction.

**Psychographics:** Sees time as the scarce resource; anything that adds
friction gets deleted immediately. Wants a tool that gets out of the way.

**Decision Factors:** Keyboard-first speed, works offline, exports/owns their
data, no forced account creation to try it.

**Language Patterns:** "Just let me add a task and move on." "If I have to
think about the tool, it's already failed." "Can I get my data out if I leave?"

**Top Objections:** "Looks like it wants to be a whole system I have to
maintain." "Login wall before I've even tried it." "Feature bloat."

## Segment 3: Screen-Reader / Low-Vision User (accessibility) (n=200, weight=0.2)
**Profile:** Navigates entirely by screen reader (VoiceOver/NVDA) and keyboard,
no mouse. Has been burned by "accessible-looking" apps that are actually unusable
with assistive tech.

**Psychographics:** Evaluates a product on whether it was actually tested with
a screen reader, not just whether it "looks clean." Distrusts marketing claims
about accessibility until proven by using it.

**Decision Factors:** Proper heading structure, labeled buttons and form fields,
status changes announced (not color/icon only), full keyboard operability.

**Language Patterns:** "Does this actually work with VoiceOver or did they just
say it does?" "An unlabeled icon button is a dead end for me." "If a status only
shows as a color change, I never know it happened."

**Top Objections:** "Most apps say 'accessible' and aren't." "One unlabeled button
can block an entire task." "I've abandoned tools mid-task because nothing was
announced."
